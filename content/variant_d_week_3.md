# Session 18 — "Extending the time"

**Day:** 18
**Duration:** ~5 min audio for 20-22 min self-directed practice
**Type:** Self-directed practice
**Goal:** Second session. Same structure as Day 16 but extending the time at moderate arousal.

Today, the second session of this kind.

[PAUSE 2s]

Same constraints as the first. Lighter grip baseline. No visual stimulus. Varied pace. Twenty to twenty-two minutes.

[PAUSE 3s]

The new instruction for today.

[PAUSE 2s]

Once you reach moderate arousal — call it a four or five on the scale — try to stay there for as long as you can. Don't let yourself climb past it. Use the pace variation and brief pauses to maintain the level.

[PAUSE 2s]

Your goal isn't to reach high arousal. Your goal is sustained moderate arousal — five or ten minutes at the same level — without progressing.

[PAUSE 3s]

Why this matters.

[PAUSE 2s]

The nervous system pattern we're reshaping is one where arousal climbs quickly and continuously toward release. Sustained moderate arousal — without progression — is the opposite shape. Time spent in that shape rewires the expectation.

[PAUSE 2s]

In real partnered sex, you spend time at moderate arousal naturally — there are pauses, position changes, slow stretches. Your nervous system needs to recognize this as sustainable, not as an interruption to be overcome.

[PAUSE 3s]

What to expect.

[PAUSE 2s]

Holding at moderate arousal is harder than letting it climb. The instinct is to push toward more. Resist the instinct.

[PAUSE 2s]

If you find yourself climbing past moderate, slow down. Pause briefly. Return to slower pace. Don't try to back off all the way to four — just maintain at five if you can.

[PAUSE 2s]

The skill of staying at five or six without progression is the same skill we'll formalize in week six as stop-start practice. Today is the informal version.

[PAUSE 3s]

Same outcomes are fine — finish, don't finish, get there, don't get there. The duration at moderate arousal is what counts.

# Session 20 — "Adding the cycle"

**Day:** 20
**Duration:** ~6 min audio for 20-22 min self-directed practice
**Type:** Self-directed practice
**Goal:** Third solo session this week. Adds the basic stop-start cycle to the variables we've been working on.

---

Today, the third solo session of the week. Adding one new element.

[PAUSE 2s]

The basic stop-start cycle.

[PAUSE 3s]

Setup. Same as the previous two sessions. Lighter grip baseline. No visual stimulus. Varied pace. Twenty to twenty-two minutes.

[PAUSE 3s]

The new structure.

[PAUSE 2s]

Three minutes of breath.

[PAUSE 2s]

Then build to moderate arousal — five or six. Hold there for thirty seconds. Use varied pace and brief pauses to maintain.

[PAUSE 2s]

Then deliberately stop physical stimulation completely. Hands off. Three slow exhales. Wait until arousal drops back down — probably to a three or four within a minute.

[PAUSE 2s]

Then resume. Build back to five or six. Hold. Stop. Wait. Repeat.

[PAUSE 2s]

Aim for three full cycles in the session. Then either finish or stop without finishing.

[PAUSE 3s]

This is the canonical stop-start technique that you'll be using more formally throughout the rest of the program. Today is your introduction.

[PAUSE 3s]

What to expect.

[PAUSE 2s]

The stops feel like more than they are. Stopping at the moment when you'd normally keep going feels counterintuitive. Some part of you will want to push through.

[PAUSE 2s]

Stop anyway. Hands off completely. The point of the stop is feeling arousal drop in the absence of stimulation, not just slowing the rate of climb.

[PAUSE 3s]

You may misjudge timing. You may think you're at a five and find you're closer to seven. You may stop too late and finish during the back-off, or too early and not really feel the cycle. All of this is normal first-session behavior.

[PAUSE 3s]

By the third cycle in the session, the pattern usually starts to make sense. Build, hold, stop, wait, build again. Each cycle is teaching the body the rhythm.
