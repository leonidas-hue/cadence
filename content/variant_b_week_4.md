# Variant B — Week 4: Releasing During Arousal

**Profile:** Hypertonic Pelvic Floor PE
**Week 4 theme:** *The skill specific to your profile: staying released while aroused.*
**Total sessions:** 7
**Average duration:** 5–9 minutes audio + self-directed practice on session days
**Voice direction:** Same Adam / Bella. Slow and unhurried. The application work this week is genuinely novel for the user — they've never deliberately practiced staying released during arousal — so the voice has to provide reassurance without rushing.

**Critical safety reminder:** The Variant B stop-start back-off is fundamentally different from other variants. The back-off is **release** — actively reverse-Kegel the floor downward — not clench-and-stop. This week's practices must reinforce this every time.

**Content philosophy:** Three solo practice sessions this week, each with the specific instruction to stay released through the climb. The user has never done this before — even in their previous solo sessions, the floor was likely gripping at moments of high arousal without their conscious awareness. This week is about bringing that awareness online and practicing the alternative.

# Session 22 — "Inverted stop-start"

**Day:** 22
**Duration:** ~7 min
**Type:** Foundation lesson
**Goal:** Frame the unique-to-B approach. Why the standard stop-start framing is wrong for hypertonic users.

---

[soft]
Welcome to week four.

[PAUSE 3s]

This week you start applying release work to arousal.

[PAUSE 2s]

Until now, the release practice has been in low-arousal contexts. Lying in constructive rest. Body scans. Diaphragmatic breathing. The floor has practiced releasing in calm states.

[PAUSE 2s]

This week we add the harder version. Releasing the floor while aroused.

[PAUSE 3s]

Let me set this up carefully because the framing matters.

[PAUSE 3s]

You may have come across stop-start technique elsewhere. The standard description goes like this: when you approach the edge during sex, stop physical stimulation, breathe, wait until arousal drops, resume. Most resources for PE recommend it. It's well-validated.

[PAUSE 2s]

Here's the catch for your profile.

[PAUSE 2s]

When most men do the stop-start back-off, they unconsciously *clench* the pelvic floor as part of the back-off. Sometimes deliberately — some resources actually recommend this — and sometimes automatically, as the body's natural response to "I need to delay ejaculation."

[PAUSE 2s]

For acquired-PE profiles, the clench is mostly neutral. The floor isn't already tight, so a brief clench during back-off doesn't reinforce a chronic pattern.

[PAUSE 2s]

For your profile, the clench is exactly wrong.

[PAUSE 3s]

Your floor is already gripped at baseline. When you add a deliberate clench during high arousal, you're stacking tension on tension. Three things can happen.

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
First — the floor briefly delays ejaculation through tension, but the stacked clench reinforces the chronic hypertonic pattern. The next session, the baseline is even tighter than it was.

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
Second — counterintuitively, the clench can actually trigger faster ejaculation. Hypertonic pelvic floors, when further tensed at high arousal, can fire the reflex earlier rather than later. The strategy backfires.

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
Third — even if the immediate session goes okay, the pattern reinforces itself for the long term. You're treating arousal management as a tightening exercise. The floor learns: high arousal means clench. Months of this and the hypertonic baseline gets worse, not better.

[PAUSE 3s]

The Variant B approach is the inverse.

[PAUSE 2s]

When you approach seven during solo work — and eventually during partnered sex — the back-off is release, not clench.

[PAUSE 2s]

Stop physical stimulation.

[PAUSE 2s]

Breathe out long and slow.

[PAUSE 2s]

And actively reverse-Kegel — let the floor sink downward, lengthen, soften.

[PAUSE 2s]

The combination — stop, exhale, release — pulls arousal down without engaging the gripping pattern. In fact, it does the opposite. It reinforces the *opposite* pattern: high arousal means open.

[PAUSE 3s]

This is the most important reframe in the entire protocol for your profile.

[PAUSE 2s]

Most other PE programs and resources won't give you this framing. They're written for the dominant profile, not yours. If you go searching online during this protocol, you'll find advice that works against you. Stay with what we're doing here.

[PAUSE 3s]

What this week looks like.

[PAUSE 2s]

Day twenty-three — first solo practice of release-through-arousal.
Day twenty-four — reflection on what you noticed.
Day twenty-five — second practice with breath integration.
Day twenty-six — continuing physical practice.
Day twenty-seven — third practice extending the duration.
Day twenty-eight — closing the week.

[PAUSE 3s]

# Session 23 — "First release-through-arousal session"

**Day:** 23
**Duration:** ~7 min audio for ~15 min self-directed practice
**Type:** Self-directed practice with audio setup
**Goal:** First explicit solo session focused on staying released as arousal climbs.

---

Today's practice. Listen now, do later when you have privacy and time.

[PAUSE 3s]

Setup.

[PAUSE 2s]

Set aside fifteen minutes. Privacy. Comfortable position — lying on your back is best for this work, since it's the position where you've been doing the reverse Kegel practice. The body has learned to release in this position; the practice transfers from there first.

[PAUSE 3s]

Three minutes of breath to start. Diaphragmatic breath, the pattern from week one. Belly rises on the inhale, falls on the exhale.

[PAUSE 3s]

Then begin solo arousal work. With one specific job — at every moment, monitor the pelvic floor and keep it released.

[PAUSE 2s]

This is going to be harder than it sounds. As arousal builds, the floor wants to engage. It's been doing this for years; you've never asked it not to before. The first session will probably involve catching the floor mid-grip multiple times and consciously letting it release.

[PAUSE 3s]

The instructions for the session.

[PAUSE 2s]

Light grip. Slow pace. Don't try to climb the arousal scale fast. Stay in the three-to-five range for the first ten minutes.

[PAUSE 2s]

Throughout, periodically check — is my floor released? If yes, continue. If you've caught it gripping, do a deliberate reverse Kegel. Inhale, lengthen the floor downward, exhale, return to neutral. Then continue the session with the floor in the released state.

[PAUSE 3s]

The session is over when fifteen minutes have passed. Whether you finish or not is fine — the goal isn't ejaculation or non-ejaculation. The goal is the practice of catching the grip and releasing.

[PAUSE 3s]

What to expect.

[PAUSE 2s]

The first session, most men with your profile catch the floor gripping ten or twenty times. Each catch is a rep. Each release is a rep.

[PAUSE 2s]

The arousal level may stay lower than your usual. Releasing the floor while aroused can actually cool the climb slightly — that's expected. We're not optimizing for high arousal in this session; we're optimizing for awareness.

[PAUSE 2s]

Some men, the first time, find the practice strange. The floor wants to grip; you're consciously releasing it. There's a kind of tension between automatic and deliberate. Stay with it.

[PAUSE 3s]

Some men also find the session surprisingly different from their usual experience. Slower-building, less driven, more spread out across the body. If this happens, notice it. The released floor changes the texture of arousal.

[PAUSE 3s]

After the session, take thirty seconds and notice. Where in the session did the grip show up most? Around what arousal level? In what context?

[PAUSE 3s]

Tomorrow, reflection.

[PAUSE 2s]

Good work today.

# Session 25 — "Adding breath integration"

**Day:** 25
**Duration:** ~6 min audio for ~18 min self-directed practice
**Type:** Self-directed practice
**Goal:** Second session, with explicit diaphragmatic breath integration throughout.

---

Today, the second session. With one addition.

[PAUSE 2s]

Diaphragmatic breath, sustained, throughout the session.

[PAUSE 3s]

Last time, you focused on catching and releasing the floor as arousal climbed. The breath was implicit — going on in the background.

[PAUSE 2s]

Today, breath is explicit. Active. Diaphragmatic — belly rising on the inhale, falling on the exhale — for the entire session, not just during the back-offs.

[PAUSE 3s]

Why this matters.

[PAUSE 2s]

The diaphragm and the pelvic floor move together. When the diaphragm drops on each inhale, the floor descends slightly with it. Sustained diaphragmatic breath provides ongoing micro-releases of the floor, throughout the session.

[PAUSE 2s]

Combined with conscious release when grip is caught, the breath provides a continuous baseline of openness. The floor stays meaningfully more released across the whole session, not just at moments of conscious intervention.

[PAUSE 3s]

Setup.

[PAUSE 2s]

Same as Day 23. Lying on your back. Privacy. Eighteen minutes.

[PAUSE 2s]

Three minutes of diaphragmatic breath to start. Drop into the pattern.

[PAUSE 2s]

Then begin solo arousal work — light grip, slow pace, three-to-five range.

[PAUSE 2s]

Maintain diaphragmatic breath throughout. If you notice the breath becoming shallow or chest-only, return to diaphragmatic.

[PAUSE 2s]

If the floor grips, do a deliberate reverse Kegel and continue.

[PAUSE 3s]

A specific note.

[PAUSE 2s]

Many men, when they get aroused, automatically shift to chest breathing. The breath becomes faster, shallower, more concentrated in the upper body. This shallow breath both contributes to and reflects sympathetic activation — and it pulls the diaphragm up, which lifts the floor with it.

[PAUSE 2s]

Sustained diaphragmatic breath during arousal is harder than at rest precisely because the body wants to shift to shallow. Catching the shift — and returning to diaphragmatic — is part of what we're practicing today.

[PAUSE 3s]

Same as before — finish or don't finish, both fine. The goal is breath sustained through arousal.

[PAUSE 3s]

Tomorrow, regular daily practice.

[PAUSE 2s]
