# Variant C — Week 4: Reverse Kegels

**Profile:** Lifelong / Hypotonic PE
**Week 4 theme:** *The other half of pelvic floor control — relaxation, not just contraction.*
**Total sessions:** 7
**Average duration:** 5–8 minutes
**Voice direction:** Same Adam / Bella. Continued patient, mechanical tone. The user has been doing 21 days of work; the voice can be slightly more direct, less reassuring.

**Content philosophy:** Reverse Kegels are the lengthening-and-relaxing counterpart to standard Kegels. For lifelong/hypotonic PE, both are useful — full control means being able to clench *and* release on demand. Most of the week is teaching the skill, then integrating it into the existing stop-start practice. Stop-start sessions continue at the rhythm established in week 3.

# Session 22 — "The other half of control"

**Day:** 22
**Duration:** ~6 min
**Type:** Foundation lesson
**Goal:** Frame reverse Kegels conceptually. Why they matter, what they're not (not the opposite of regular Kegels in any harmful way), and how they'll integrate with existing work.

---

[soft]
Welcome to week four.

[PAUSE 2s]

This week we add reverse Kegels.

[PAUSE 3s]

Until now, all your pelvic floor work has been contraction. Standard Kegels — squeezing the muscle, holding briefly, releasing. We've trained slow contractions and quick contractions. The muscle has gotten stronger.

[PAUSE 2s]

Reverse Kegels are the opposite skill. Instead of contracting, you actively lengthen and relax the muscle below its normal resting baseline. It's a deliberate downward release.

[PAUSE 3s]

Why this matters.

[PAUSE 2s]

Full control of any muscle group means being able to engage *and* disengage on command. Most men can clench their pelvic floor — that's automatic. Far fewer can deliberately relax it below baseline. The relaxation skill takes specific training.

[PAUSE 2s]

For your profile, the relaxation skill matters in two specific contexts.

[PAUSE 3s]

First — during the back-off in stop-start practice. When you stop physical stimulation and want arousal to drop fast, the pelvic floor often holds a low-level tension that maintains some sympathetic activation. A deliberate reverse Kegel during the back-off pulls the system down faster than breath alone.

[PAUSE 2s]

Second — during partnered sex itself. Many men, in moments of high arousal, unconsciously tense the pelvic floor. The tension feeds the reflex. A deliberate release at high arousal — done quickly, quietly, internally — can buy you several seconds of additional control.

[PAUSE 3s]

What reverse Kegels are *not*.

[PAUSE 2s]

They're not the same as bearing down. Bearing down is what you do during a bowel movement — engaging the abdominal wall to push through the floor. Reverse Kegels do not engage the abdominal wall. They release the floor itself, without pushing.

[PAUSE 2s]

A specific safety note. For users with active hemorrhoids, recent prostate surgery, or a hernia history, reverse Kegels should be approached cautiously and ideally cleared with a physician first. The pressure dynamics around the pelvis can interact with these conditions. If any of those apply to you, talk to a doctor before proceeding with this week's work.

[PAUSE 3s]

What this week looks like.

[PAUSE 2s]

Day twenty-three — finding the reverse Kegel. Body literacy work, similar to how we found the contraction in week one.

[PAUSE 2s]

Day twenty-four — first dedicated reverse Kegel practice.

[PAUSE 2s]

Days twenty-five through twenty-seven — integration into stop-start. Stop-start sessions this week include the reverse Kegel during back-off. Two to three stop-start sessions across the week as usual.

[PAUSE 2s]

Day twenty-eight — closing the week.

[PAUSE 3s]

[soft]

# Session 23 — "Finding the reverse"

**Day:** 23
**Duration:** ~7 min
**Type:** Practice (PFM literacy, reverse direction)
**Goal:** Help the user identify the reverse Kegel sensation. This is harder than finding the standard contraction — most men have never deliberately tried to engage the lengthening of the pelvic floor.

---

Today, finding the reverse Kegel.

[PAUSE 2s]

This is more subtle than the contraction we worked on in week one. The contraction is something most men have done at some point, even if accidentally. The deliberate release is foreign — most men have never tried to consciously do it.

[PAUSE 3s]

Lie down. Comfortable position. Three slow breaths to settle.

[PAUSE 1s]

[soft]
In.
[BREATH IN — 4s]
Out.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[soft]
In.
[BREATH IN — 4s]
Out.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[soft]
In.
[BREATH IN — 4s]
Out.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[PAUSE 3s]

First, contract the pelvic floor — same as you've been doing. Light effort. Up and in. Hold for two seconds.

[PAUSE 4s]

Release. Fully. Let it drop completely.

[PAUSE 5s]

Now, from that fully-released position, try to release further.

[PAUSE 3s]

Imagine you're letting the pelvic floor sink, lengthen, expand downward. As if there's a muscular fan inside the pelvis that's been slightly furled, and you're letting it open out gently.

[PAUSE 5s]

Some men describe it as a feeling of mild downward pressure, but without the abdominal push of a bowel movement. Others describe it as a feeling of warmth or expansion in the perineum. Others feel almost nothing at first — and that's also normal.

[PAUSE 3s]

A specific cue that helps.

[PAUSE 2s]

On your next exhale, instead of holding the floor neutral, let the breath move down through it. The diaphragm and pelvic floor are connected — when the diaphragm drops on inhale, the floor descends slightly; when the diaphragm rises on exhale, the floor often rises with it. We're going to use that connection to learn the deliberate release.

[PAUSE 3s]

[soft]
Inhale, four counts. Let your belly expand.
[BREATH IN — 4s]

[soft]
On the exhale — and this is the key — instead of letting the floor lift back to neutral, deliberately let it stay sunk. Or even sink slightly more.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[PAUSE 3s]

[soft]
Try again. Inhale.
[BREATH IN — 4s]

[soft]
Exhale, floor stays sunk.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[PAUSE 3s]

[soft]
One more. Inhale.
[BREATH IN — 4s]

[soft]
Exhale. Floor lengthens, opens.
[BREATH OUT — 6s]

[PAUSE 5s]

That sensation — whatever you noticed — is what we're cultivating. Some men feel it strongly the first time. Most don't, and find it gets clearer over four or five days of practice.

[PAUSE 3s]

A specific check.

[PAUSE 2s]

Place a hand on your lower belly. When you do the reverse Kegel correctly, your belly should not push outward forcefully. There may be a tiny amount of expansion, but it's subtle. If your belly is pushing out hard, you're bearing down — that's not what we want.

[PAUSE 2s]

Place another hand at the perineum, between scrotum and anus. When you do the reverse correctly, you may feel a slight relaxation or warmth there — the muscle softening below your hand.

[PAUSE 3s]

For today, practice this gently. Three or four cycles, no more. The reverse Kegel is a subtle skill — over-practicing it on the first day produces no extra benefit. Gentle practice, daily, is what builds it.

[PAUSE 3s]

[PAUSE 2s]

# Session 25 — "Integrating into stop-start"

**Day:** 25
**Duration:** ~6 min audio for ~20 min self-directed practice
**Type:** Self-directed practice
**Goal:** First stop-start session that incorporates reverse Kegels during the back-off.

---

Today, the first stop-start session with reverse Kegels integrated.

[PAUSE 2s]

You've been doing stop-start for ten days. The basic cycle is familiar — climb to six, stop, breathe, drop to four, resume.

[PAUSE 2s]

Today we add the reverse Kegel to the back-off phase.

[PAUSE 3s]

The new structure.

[PAUSE 2s]

When you stop, do three things in sequence:

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
First — hands off, fully stop physical stimulation.

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
Second — exhale, long and slow. Four-eight breath.

[PAUSE 2s]

[firmer]
Third — on each exhale, do a deliberate reverse Kegel. Let the floor lengthen and stay open through the breath.

[PAUSE 3s]

What this combination does.

[PAUSE 2s]

The exhale alone pulls heart rate down via vagal tone. The reverse Kegel adds a direct pelvic-floor relaxation, which reduces the floor's contribution to sympathetic activation. Together, they pull arousal down measurably faster than either alone.

[PAUSE 2s]

In practice, your back-off phase will probably go from thirty to forty-five seconds (with breath alone) to twenty to thirty seconds (with breath plus reverse). Smaller window of pause. More cycles per session if you want them.

[PAUSE 3s]

The session structure.

[PAUSE 2s]

Twenty minutes total. Three minutes of breath to start. Five climb-hold-back-off cycles. Each cycle: climb to six, hold for fifteen to thirty seconds, stop, breath plus reverse Kegel until back at four, resume.

[PAUSE 2s]

Then either finish or stop without finishing.

[PAUSE 3s]

A note on the combination.

[PAUSE 2s]

If running breath and reverse Kegel together feels like too much to coordinate, simplify. For the first session, prioritize the breath. Add the reverse Kegel when the breath is comfortable. Two or three sessions, the combination becomes natural.

[PAUSE 3s]

Tomorrow's a non-practice day. Reflection.

[PAUSE 2s]

Good work today.

**Type:** Self-directed practice
**Goal:** Final stop-start session of the week. Same structure as Day 25 but with one extension — a single attempt at staying at six for forty-five seconds before back-off.

---

Today, the final stop-start session of the week.

[PAUSE 2s]

Same structure as Wednesday's. Three minutes of breath. Five climb-hold-back-off cycles. Breath and reverse Kegel during back-off. Twenty to twenty-five minutes total.

[PAUSE 2s]

One extension today.

[PAUSE 2s]

In one of the five cycles — pick whichever feels right, probably the second or third — try to extend the hold at six to forty-five seconds. Up from fifteen to thirty.

[PAUSE 3s]

Why this extension matters.

[PAUSE 2s]

Real partnered sex doesn't have nice clean back-off cycles every thirty seconds. There are stretches of high arousal where you stay at six or seven for longer. The skill of holding at six for extended periods is what eventually translates to those stretches.

[PAUSE 2s]

Don't try to extend all five cycles to forty-five seconds. One. The others stay at the shorter hold. Forty-five-second holds are fatiguing — both physically and attentionally.

[PAUSE 3s]

A specific cue for the extended hold.

[PAUSE 2s]

During the extended hold, use the reverse Kegel intermittently — even while continuing physical stimulation. A subtle relaxation downward, every ten or fifteen seconds, helps prevent the upward drift toward seven.

[PAUSE 2s]

This is the move you'll eventually use during partnered sex. Internal, invisible, mechanical. Maintains six against the natural drift upward.
