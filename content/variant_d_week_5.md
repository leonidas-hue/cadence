Why two weeks of zero.

[PAUSE 2s]

The two-week duration is a design choice for this protocol — short enough to be doable, long enough that the nervous system has a sustained period without the high-stimulus input. We don't have clean clinical data on the optimal duration for this kind of taper, since the diagnostic category itself isn't well-established in the published literature. Two weeks is what makes sense given everything else we're trying to hold together — long enough to produce a felt shift for most users, short enough that it doesn't become an endurance test.

[PAUSE 2s]

Two weeks is calibrated to be meaningful but bounded. After it ends, what you do is your decision based on the data the experiment produces.

[PAUSE 3s]

What to expect.

[PAUSE 2s]

The first three to four days of zero use are usually the hardest. Cravings peak. The contexts that previously triggered usage will trigger more strongly than they did during the reduction phase, because the safety valve of "I can use later this week" is gone.

[PAUSE 2s]

After day four or five, for most men, the cravings start to subside. Not gone — but less acute. Background noise rather than acute pulls.

[PAUSE 2s]

By the end of week six, many men report a different relationship to the question — less pull, more clarity about what porn was doing for them, sometimes a felt sense of recalibration.

[PAUSE 3s]

The plan for cravings.

[PAUSE 2s]

Have your alternatives ready before you need them. The moments you've identified as high-craving — specific times of day, specific contexts — should have pre-decided responses. Not "I'll figure it out when it happens." Decided in advance.

[PAUSE 2s]

The thirty-second sequence from last week still applies — name the craving, four-eight breath, notice what's underneath. Practice using it. The more often you use it during reduction, the more reliable it is during zero use.
