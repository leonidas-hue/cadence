# Shared — IELT Logging Tutorial

**Type:** Short tutorial
**Used by:** All variants (Week 1, Day 7 or as part of intake)
**Duration:** ~3 min
**Voice direction:** Same Adam / Bella. Direct. Brief. This is reference content the user may replay when they need to remember the protocol.

This tutorial explains what IELT is, how to measure it solo and partnered, and the recommended logging frequency.

---

## Script

Quick tutorial. How to measure IELT.

[PAUSE 2s]

IELT stands for *intravaginal ejaculation latency time*. Despite the name, the concept applies to any partnered penetrative sex. It's the time from the start of penetration to ejaculation.

[PAUSE 2s]

For research and clinical use, IELT is the gold-standard outcome metric for PE work. We track it because it's objective and comparable over time.

[PAUSE 3s]

How to measure it.

[PAUSE 2s]

For partnered sex, ideally a partner uses a stopwatch. Discreet phone timer. Start when penetration begins, stop when ejaculation occurs. Most partners are willing to do this once or twice if you ask.

[PAUSE 2s]

If a partner stopwatch isn't available, estimate. Mark it in the app within an hour after the session, while the experience is fresh. Approximate is fine — 1 minute, 2 minutes, 3-5 minutes. The exact number matters less than the trend.

[PAUSE 2s]

For solo work — masturbation — measure from the start of consistent stimulation to ejaculation. Less directly applicable to partnered timing, but useful as a parallel data point if you don't have a partner currently.

[PAUSE 3s]

How often to measure.

[PAUSE 2s]

Once a month is enough. Maybe every two weeks if you really want.

[PAUSE 2s]

Don't measure weekly. The data is too noisy at small scales — a single session influences the average too much. Worse, weekly measuring creates pressure that works against the protocol.

[PAUSE 2s]

The first six to eight weeks of behavioral training rarely show big metric movement. Real improvement usually shows up at week six to eight or later. Measure when you start, then once at weeks four, eight, and twelve. That's enough.

[PAUSE 3s]

A note on what counts as success.

[PAUSE 2s]

Going from one minute to four minutes is clinically significant. So is going from two minutes to six. The benchmark for clinical PE recovery is typically getting above three to four minutes consistently — not extending duration indefinitely.

[PAUSE 2s]

Above five to seven minutes, additional duration becomes less correlated with satisfaction. Once you're in that range, the work shifts from extending duration to deepening the experience.

[PAUSE 3s]

Log entries are private. Stored on your device. Not synced anywhere unless you choose to.

[PAUSE 3s]

That's it. Use the timer button when you want, log the result, check trends monthly.

[PAUSE 2s]

Done.
