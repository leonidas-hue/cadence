# Variant B — Week 2: Extended Release

**Profile:** Hypertonic Pelvic Floor PE
**Week 2 theme:** *Going deeper into release. The patterns that maintain the clench.*
**Total sessions:** 7
**Average duration:** 6–9 minutes
**Voice direction:** Same Adam / Bella. Slightly warmer than Variant A or C — the user's nervous system is already wound tight; the voice should not add pressure. Slow, patient, unhurried.

**Critical safety reminders:**

- **No standard Kegels. No quick contractions. No strength work.** This is a release-focused protocol throughout.
- The only "contractions" permitted are brief muscle-tagging exercises where contraction is used to identify the muscle for subsequent release.
- Pelvic floor PT recommendation is repeated.
- Watch for tonal pressure that could trigger the user's stress response — the voice has to be unhurried, lest we feed the very tension we're trying to release.

**Content philosophy:** Week 2 deepens the work from week 1. The reverse Kegel is now the foundational practice; we add active relaxation postures (constructive rest, supine butterfly, etc.) and body scan focused specifically on the muscle groups that co-tighten with the pelvic floor — jaw, abdominals, inner thighs. The user should leave week 2 with a clearer felt sense of what released-floor feels like, and which other body parts they were unconsciously gripping in concert.

[PAUSE 2s]

[PAUSE 5s]

[PAUSE 2s]

[PAUSE 2s]

# Session 11 — "Active relaxation postures"

**Day:** 11
**Duration:** ~9 min
**Type:** Practice (postural release)
**Goal:** Introduce two specific postures that mechanically release the pelvic floor and connected groups.

---

Today, two postures that mechanically support pelvic floor release.

[PAUSE 2s]

These aren't yoga poses — though they look like yoga poses. They're specific positions where gravity and joint geometry put the floor in a maximally lengthened state. Holding the position lets the floor release further than it can release in standing or sitting.

[PAUSE 3s]

Posture one. Constructive rest with elevated feet.

[PAUSE 2s]

Lie on your back. Bend your knees and rest your feet on a chair seat, a couch cushion, or a stack of pillows — whatever is available. The chair height should put your knees at roughly a 90-degree angle above your hips.

[PAUSE 4s]

This position passively decompresses the lower spine and lets the pelvis settle into a neutral position. The pelvic floor, in this position, is in its most lengthened state. Hold this for five to seven minutes, breathing diaphragmatically.

[PAUSE 3s]

Set a timer if you want, or just rest until you feel the body fully settle.

[PAUSE 5s]

While you're here, run through the four zones from yesterday's body scan. Jaw soft. Belly soft. Inner thighs heavy. Diaphragm dropping. And the floor lengthening with each inhale.

[PAUSE 5s]

Stay in the pose for five to seven minutes. I'll continue the audio with the second posture afterward.

[PAUSE 3s]

(Audio cues a soft chime to indicate the user should now move to the second pose, after sufficient time has elapsed. In production, this can be a separate audio segment.)

[PAUSE 5s]

[soft]
When you're ready, the second posture.

[PAUSE 3s]

Posture two. Supine butterfly.

[PAUSE 2s]

Stay on your back. Bring the soles of your feet together so they touch, and let your knees fall outward. Feet about six to twelve inches from your pelvis — comfortable distance.

[PAUSE 4s]

If your knees don't fall easily outward — if there's significant tension in the hips or inner thighs — place pillows or rolled blankets under each knee to support them. The pose should feel passive, not stretchy. We're not stretching; we're letting things release.

[PAUSE 3s]

Arms at your sides. Hands resting on your belly or pelvis if you want.

[PAUSE 4s]

In this position, the inner thighs are passively lengthened — meaning the adductors that often co-tighten with the floor get a sustained release. The pelvic floor, in concert, also lengthens.

[PAUSE 3s]

Three to five minutes here. Breathing diaphragmatically. Same four zones.

[PAUSE 5s]

A specific instruction for this pose. If you notice your inner thighs gripping — pulling the knees back toward each other — that's the unconscious clench in a different muscle group. Gently let them go.

[PAUSE 5s]

Stay with this pose for three to five minutes. Then come out slowly — bring your knees back together with your hands, then take a few breaths before sitting up.

[PAUSE 3s]

These two postures together take about ten minutes. Use them three times this week. Ideally in the evening — they support sleep too, because they shift the nervous system toward parasympathetic.

[PAUSE 3s]

[PAUSE 2s]

# Session 12 — "Hip mobility, expanded"

**Day:** 12
**Duration:** ~7 min
**Type:** Practice (hip mobility)
**Goal:** Build on week 1's hip mobility work. Add two more positions.

---

Today, expanded hip mobility.

[PAUSE 2s]

Last week's three positions — child's pose with wide knees, happy baby, figure four — stay in rotation. Today we add two more.

[PAUSE 3s]

The hip joint and the pelvic floor share fascia. Tightness in the hips often co-presents with hypertonic floor, and addressing one helps the other. We're not chasing flexibility for its own sake — we're loosening the connective tissue around the pelvis so the floor has room to release.

[PAUSE 3s]

Position four. Pigeon pose, modified.

[PAUSE 2s]

Get on your hands and knees. Bring your right knee forward and place it behind your right wrist. Your right shin can be perpendicular to your body or angled in toward the opposite side, whichever is comfortable.

[PAUSE 4s]

Slide your left leg back behind you, keeping the toes pointing back, the leg straight. Your hips should be roughly squared toward the floor.

[PAUSE 3s]

If your right hip lifts significantly off the floor — most men's do, the first time — place a folded blanket or pillow under your right buttock to support it. The pose should be sustainable, not strained.

[PAUSE 4s]

Lower your torso forward — onto forearms, or all the way to the floor if comfortable. Rest your forehead on your hands or the floor.

[PAUSE 4s]

This pose targets the right hip's external rotators. These muscles directly affect pelvic floor tone — when they're chronically tight, they pull the pelvis into positions that maintain floor tension.

[PAUSE 3s]

Hold for two minutes per side. Don't push for depth. The release happens in stillness, not in pressure.

[PAUSE 3s]

When you've done both sides, position five.

[PAUSE 3s]

Position five. Reclined twist, knees together.

[PAUSE 2s]

Lie on your back. Bring both knees in toward your chest. Let your knees fall to one side — to the right, say — while keeping your shoulders flat on the floor. Your head can stay neutral or turn the opposite way.

[PAUSE 4s]

This is a passive twist. The knees go where gravity takes them. If they don't reach the floor, that's fine — place a pillow under them for support.

[PAUSE 3s]

Hold for two minutes per side.

[PAUSE 3s]

The twist releases the deep low-back muscles and the obliques. Both can hold tension that translates to the floor.

[PAUSE 3s]

These five positions — three from week one, two new ones — make up your mobility set. Three sessions per week. Pick three positions per session, rotating which ones you use, so each gets attention across the week.

[PAUSE 3s]

Tomorrow, an integrated session that pulls everything together.

[PAUSE 2s]

Good work today.
