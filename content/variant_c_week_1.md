# Variant C — Week 1: Building the Foundation

**Profile:** Lifelong / Hypotonic PE
**Week 1 theme:** *Building what's never been built.*
**Total sessions:** 7 (one per day, with same Day 1 → Day 2 immediate-unlock pattern)
**Average duration:** 6–9 minutes
**Voice direction:** Same Adam / Bella voice. Tone is direct, honest, slightly more matter-of-fact than Variants A and B. This user has often had PE since their first sexual experiences and may have tried other things. They don't want to be coddled — they want clarity. The voice respects that.

**Content philosophy:** This profile is the most neurobiological — the wiring tilts toward fast ejaculation, and behavioral training takes longer to override that wiring than for acquired profiles. We have to be honest about timeline (8–12 weeks for behavioral change, sometimes longer) and unembarrassed about pharma options (dapoxetine, topical lidocaine) as legitimate bridges while behavioral skills build. The biggest risk is the user expecting Variant A speed (4–6 weeks) and quitting at week 5 because they haven't seen Variant A results.

# Session 2 — "Finding the muscle"

**Day:** 2
**Duration:** ~8 min
**Type:** Practice (PFM literacy)
**Goal:** Help the user identify the pelvic floor muscle correctly. Many men contract the wrong muscles when they try to do Kegels (glutes, abs, inner thighs). Day 2 is just literacy — not strength.

---

Today, finding the muscle.

[PAUSE 2s]

Before we do any pelvic floor strengthening, we need to make sure you're contracting the right muscle. This sounds obvious. It is not. Most men who try Kegels for the first time accidentally engage their glutes, their abs, or their inner thighs instead.

[PAUSE 2s]

If you train the wrong muscle, you waste weeks of work and wonder why nothing changes. So we spend a session on this.

[PAUSE 3s]

Get comfortable. Sitting in a chair works fine. Both feet flat on the floor.

[PAUSE 4s]

Place one hand on your lower belly, just above the pubic bone. Place the other hand on your inner thigh.

[PAUSE 3s]

Now try to contract your pelvic floor. Imagine you're trying to stop the flow of urine, or trying to stop yourself from passing gas. Hold for two seconds.

[PAUSE 2s]

Do it now.

[PAUSE 4s]

Release.

[PAUSE 3s]

Check your hands. Did your belly hand feel a hard contraction? Did your inner thigh feel tightening? If yes, you're recruiting the wrong muscles.

[PAUSE 3s]

The pelvic floor is deep. When you contract it correctly, both your belly and your inner thigh should feel mostly soft. There may be a tiny amount of co-activation, but it should be subtle. Most of the work is happening internally, in a place your hands cannot reach.

[PAUSE 3s]

Try again. This time, lighter effort. Maybe 30 percent of your maximum. The lighter the effort, the easier it is to isolate the right muscle.

[PAUSE 2s]

Contract — gentle.

[PAUSE 3s]

Release.

[PAUSE 4s]

Better?

[PAUSE 3s]

[PAUSE 2s]

Try one more time.

[PAUSE 1s]

Up and in — gentle.

[PAUSE 3s]

Release. Fully.

[PAUSE 5s]

# Session 5 — "The arousal scale"

**Day:** 5
**Duration:** ~7 min
**Type:** Lesson + reflection
**Goal:** Teach the 1–10 arousal scale framework. This is the conceptual foundation for stop-start practice in week three.

---

Today, a concept that becomes central to your protocol.

[PAUSE 2s]

The arousal scale.

[PAUSE 3s]

Sex therapists have used this for decades, and it works because it gives you a vocabulary for something most men have never thought about explicitly — *how aroused am I right now?*

[PAUSE 2s]

The scale runs from one to ten.

[PAUSE 2s]

One is no arousal at all. Walking down the street. Reading a book.

[PAUSE 1s]

Three is mild interest. The early stages — mental engagement, maybe some physical response.

[PAUSE 1s]

Five is solidly aroused. Erection, body engaged, but a long way from finishing.

[PAUSE 1s]

Seven is significantly aroused. Approaching the edge but still controllable.

[PAUSE 1s]

Eight is what therapists call point of no return adjacent. You're close. Without intervention, you're going to finish in the next minute or two.

[PAUSE 1s]

Nine is point of no return. The reflex is firing. You will finish in seconds.

[PAUSE 1s]

Ten is ejaculation.

[PAUSE 3s]

Why this scale matters. The whole skill of ejaculatory control is learning to spend more time in the five-to-seven range, and to back off before you cross into eight. Most men with your profile have one mode — they go from three to nine in a span of seconds, with nothing in between. Or they don't notice they've crossed seven until they're already at nine.

[PAUSE 3s]

The skill we're building, throughout this protocol, is awareness. Specifically, *awareness during arousal*. The ability to internally check in and say — I'm at a six right now. I'm at a seven. Time to slow down.

[PAUSE 2s]

This sounds simple. It is not. Arousal makes attention narrow. Once you're at a seven, your awareness gets pulled toward the sensation, away from any meta-monitoring. The skill takes weeks to build. We start in week three.

[PAUSE 3s]

For today, one reflection. Think back to the last few times you had sex or masturbated. Try to map your experience to this scale.

[PAUSE 2s]

Where did you spend most of the time? How long were you at five? At seven? Did you notice the eight before it became nine?

[PAUSE 5s]

Most men, when they first do this exercise, realize they've never thought about it this way. The whole experience was unmonitored. Awareness blanket-fogged.

[PAUSE 2s]

That's the starting point. Awareness comes online slowly, with deliberate practice.

[PAUSE 3s]

One more thing. When you do solo work in week three — and you will — your job will be to recognize when you've crossed seven, and to back off completely. Cooling down to four or five before approaching again. The skill of the back-off is more important than the skill of the approach.

[PAUSE 2s]

**Goal:** Connect breathwork to arousal regulation. Long exhales actively pull arousal levels down.

Today, the most reliable tool you have for managing the arousal scale we covered yesterday.

[PAUSE 2s]

Breath.

[PAUSE 2s]

Specifically, the long, slow exhale.

[PAUSE 3s]

Here's the physiology. Sexual arousal runs on the sympathetic nervous system — the same system that activates under stress, excitement, threat. Heart rate up. Breath fast and shallow. Muscles tense. As arousal climbs from five to seven to eight, your sympathetic activation climbs with it. They're inseparable.

[PAUSE 2s]

The exhale is the only direct lever you have to shift the nervous system back toward parasympathetic — toward calm. A slow, long exhale pulls heart rate down within seconds. And when heart rate drops, sympathetic activation drops. And when sympathetic activation drops, arousal level drops.
